About Pokhara

Introduction
Pokhara is a city located in Western Nepal. It's known for its natural beauty and has become a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world. Pokhara is situated at an altitude of 1,500 meters above sea level and has views of the Annapurna mountain range on one side and Phewa Lake on the other.
Pokhara was established as a town in 1950 by King Mahendra after he decided that it would be better to move the capital away from Kathmandu because it was too crowded there. The city quickly grew into an important hub for trade between India and Tibet thanks to its location along two major trading routes: one going east through Bhaktapur; another going south via Butwal-Nepalgunj (now Rupandehi).
The Mountains
The Annapurna Range is one of the most famous mountain ranges in the world, and Pokhara is located at its base. The mountains are home to some of Nepal's most breathtaking views and include some of its tallest peaks: Machhapuchhre (6,993 meters), Annapurna I (8,091 meters), Dhaulagiri I (8167 meters).
The Annapurna Himalayan range extends for about 200 kilometers across central Nepal from east to west with multiple peaks over 7000 meters high. It is part of what is known as "The Roof of the World" because it forms an almost continuous barrier between India and Tibet/China on one side with lowland plains on either side forming large basins that can be used for farming or grazing livestock such as yaks or sheep
Adventure Sports
Pokhara is also a great place for adventure sports. If you're looking for some adrenaline-pumping activities, there are plenty of options here. You can paraglide over the lake or zip-line through the trees, or even rock climb if that's what gets your blood pumping!\ Paragliding is one of my favorite things to do in Pokhara because it gives me an incredible view of Mount Machhapuchre (5th highest mountain in Nepal). It's also really easy and safe--you just sit on top of a parachute and someone pulls you up into the air with a motorized winch! Zip lining is another fun activity; this time you'll be strapped into harnesses while hanging from cables high above ground level as they zip across treetops at speeds up to 50km/hr! Rock climbing isn't quite as thrilling but still offers plenty of challenges: You'll need strength and endurance as well as good balance skills if you want to climb successfully without falling off too many times along the way!
Lakeside Attractions
Phewa Lake The most famous lake in Pokhara is Phewa Lake, which is located at the center of the city. The lake was formed by a landslide that blocked the Seti River and created a natural dam. It's surrounded by mountains, making it an ideal spot for hiking and trekking. If you're looking for something more relaxing than hiking, there are plenty of activities available here as well. You can rent paddleboats or kayaks from one of many vendors along its shoreline, take a boat ride across its waters (for a fee), or simply sit back with your feet dangling over the edge while enjoying some snacks from one of several nearby restaurants or cafes!
Cultural Experiences
The International Mountain Museum, located on the southern edge of Pokhara, is a great place to learn about Nepal's history and culture. The museum houses artifacts from all over Nepal, including clothing, jewelry and weapons used by various tribes throughout history. If you're interested in learning more about local customs and traditions then check out Mahendra Cave which has an exhibition on Buddhism as well as other religions practiced in Nepal at different times in its history.
The Seti River Gorge is another must-see attraction for anyone visiting Pokhara; this beautiful canyon was carved out by a tributary of the Kali Gandaki River over millions of years through erosion caused by waterfalls dropping into deep pools below them (the largest waterfall here drops 200 meters!). You can hike along its banks or take a boat ride down river where there are many caves that have been carved out over time by flowing water--it's quite amazing how much nature has changed these rocks!
The Local Cuisine
* Dal Bhat:
A staple of the Nepali diet, this dish consists of rice and lentils served with vegetables, meat or fish. It's usually served with a spicy tomato soup called dal baht tarkari (dal bhat with curry).
* Momos:
These steamed dumplings are made from unleavened dough filled with ground meat and/or vegetables, then steamed or deep fried until they're crispy on the outside and soft within. They're often eaten as an appetizer but can also be ordered as part of a main course consisting of several different types of momos served in broth or sauce.
* Sel Roti:
A flatbread made from whole wheat flour that is shaped into disks before being cooked on a griddle over direct heat (similar to how tortillas are made). It's used as an accompaniment for curries or stews such as daal bhaat (lentils), sel roti kheema curry ($5 USD)
Shopping
Pokhara has a number of local markets where you can pick up souvenirs and gifts for friends and family. The most famous market is the Durbar Square, which is located at the center of Pokhara city. It's here that you'll find everything from handmade jewelry to traditional Nepali clothing and handicrafts made by local artisans.
If you're looking for trekking gear or equipment like tents or sleeping bags, then check out one of the many outdoor stores in town; these shops sell everything from backpacks to sleeping mats and waterproof jackets (and even some snacks!).
Accommodations
Pokhara has a wide range of accommodation options. There are hotels, hostels and guest houses in all price ranges. If you're looking for something more luxurious, there are also resorts with private pools and other amenities such as yoga studios and spas.
Pokhara has been popular with tourists since the 1960s so there are plenty of places to stay at any budget level. The best thing about Pokhara is that most hotels offer very good value for money compared to other parts of Nepal or even other countries in South Asia (such as India).

Conclusion
Pokhara is a unique place that has something for everyone. Whether you're looking to experience the natural beauty of Nepal or immerse yourself in cultural diversity, Pokhara has it all. It's also a great place to relax and unwind after your trekking adventures!
